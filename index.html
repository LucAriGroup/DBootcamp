About Our Bootcamp

We understand the pain of seeing your child's attitude change for the worse. Our program is designed to help them see life from a different perspective.

Your Last Stop Solution

Discipline Bootcamp SA understands your suffering and pain, and we are your solution to getting your child back on track. We've created programs that will assist them to polish their attitude and see life from a different angle so they can become better members of society.

Our KZN office and Discipline Bootcamp is always open, and you can register your child at any time of the year - whether during school holidays or not. The camp is always running.

We accept males and females from age 7 to 38 years, and they will be well looked after within our bootcamp. We have assisted many young adults from all over South Africa.

Our Programs

We address various behavioral issues and provide skills development to ensure participants leave as improved individuals.

Behavioral Issues We Address

  • Laziness & lack of motivation
  • Bad attitude & disrespect
  • Drug and alcohol abuse
  • Back chatting & defiance
  • Low self-confidence
  • Peer pressure challenges
  • Leadership skills development

We have well-trained facilitators with first aid certificates and camp leaders with Outdoor & Adventure Practice Certificates, along with professionals like psychologists, social workers, and motivational speakers.

Skills Development

As part of our program, participants will learn practical skills that will benefit them after they leave the camp:

  • Basic Welding
  • Photo Editing (Photoshop)
  • Basic Cake Baking

This ensures they return not only with an improved attitude but also with a practical skill that can help them build a future.

For Severe Cases

For those who abuse drugs or alcohol, we urge parents to sign them up for longer periods:

  • 20 days minimum for substance abuse
  • 2 months up to 6 months recommended
  • 12-month programs available for severe cases

Our program is designed to provide the time needed for genuine transformation and rehabilitation.

Requirements

What your child needs to bring for their stay at the bootcamp.

Clothing & Uniform

  • Uniform fee: R250 (paid separately)
  • Plain Red T-shirt
  • Plain Black sweat pants
  • Warm clothes including pyjamas
  • Simple clothing (nothing expensive)

Bedding & Personal Items

  • A pillow and blanket (or sleeping bag)
  • Toiletries
  • A plastic cup and plastic plate
  • A pen and notebook
  • A battery torch (preferably rechargeable)

Spiritual Materials

  • A Christian Bible or Quran Bible (compulsory)

Prohibited Items

  • No electrical devices like cellphones, etc.
  • No expensive jewelry or items
  • No inappropriate clothing
  • No drugs, alcohol, or tobacco products
